A study on 2D/3D hand representation for computer vision applications

Monocular 3D Hand Pose Estimation with Implicit Camera Alignment

Christos Pantazopoulos1, Spyridon Thermos2 Gerasimos Potamianos1,
1Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Thessaly, Greece 2Moverse,
arXiv Code

Abstract

Estimating the 3D hand articulation from a single color image is a continuously investigated problem with applications in Augmented Reality (AR), Virtual Reality (VR), Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), and robotics. Apart from the absence of depth information, occlusions, articulation complexity, and the need for camera parameters knowledge pose additional challenges. In this work, we propose an optimization pipeline for estimating the 3D hand articulation from 2D keypoint input, which includes a keypoint alignment step and a fingertip loss to overcome the need to know or estimate the camera parameters. We evaluate our approach on the EgoDexter and Dexter+Object benchmarks to showcase that our approach performs competitively with the SotA, while also demonstrating its robustness when processing "in-the-wild" images without any prior camera knowledge. Our quantitative analysis highlights the sensitivity of the 2D keypoint estimation accuracy, despite the use of hand priors.
